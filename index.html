The First Executive Minister of the Iglesia Ni Cristo

ADMINISTRATION TIMELINE:

  • 1947 - Became the General Treasurer of the church.
  • 1953 - Elected as successor to Felix Y. Manalo by provincial ministers.
  • 1957 - Became the District Minister of Manila.
  • 1965 - Established the first resettlement farm (Maligaya Farm) in Palayan City, Nueva Ecija, Philippines. Similar projects were established in Cavite, Rizal and other provinces.
  • 1967 - Set his vision to overseas mission and global expansion, four years after assuming leadership role.
  • July 27, 1968 - Officiated the first worship service of the church outside the Philippines. The following month, the Executive Minister was in California to establish the San Francisco congregation and lead its inaugural worship service.
  • 1969 - A congregation was organized in Guam. INC started operating a radio station.
  • 1970's - The church first reached China by way of Hong Kong, and Japan through Tokyo.
  • 1971 - INC Central Office building was built in Quezon City. The church set foot in Canada.
  • 1972 - The first local congregation in Europe was established in England.
  • 1974 - The Ministerial Institute of Development, currently the New Era University College of Evangelical Ministry, was founded in Quiapo, Manila. It moved to its current location in Quezon City in 1978.
  • Mid 70s - The church came to Germany and Switzerland and Australia.
  • 1976 - The first congregation in Thailand was established.
  • October 1978 - The first mission in Northern Africa opened in Nigeria.
  • 1979 - The mission reached Spain and missions have already been conducted in Brunei.
  • 1983 - First television program aired.
  • July 27, 1984 - The 7,000-seater Central Temple was added in the complex.
  • 1987 - US Main Office (USMO) was set up in Daly City, California to assist the INC central administration in supervising the then 11 districts of the church in the West.
  • End of 1980's - Congregations and missions could be found in the Scandinavian countries and their neighbors.
  • 1990 - The first local congregation in Latin America was established in Guantanamo Bay, Cuba.
  • 1991 - The church reached Mexico and Aruba.
  • July 27, 1994 - Rome, Italy congregation was established
  • 1995 - The church had 4,500 students and five extension schools in Bulacan, Cavite, Laguna, Pampanga and Rizal.
  • March 31, 1996 - Jerusalem, Israel congregation was established.
  • May 10, 1997 - Athens, Greece congregation was established.
  • 2000 and beyond - Congregations rose in the Central and South American countries.
